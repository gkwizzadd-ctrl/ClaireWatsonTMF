# Claire Watson — Voice & Outreach Style

## Who Claire Is
Claire Watson is a Member Success Manager at TM Forum, responsible for onboarding and engaging telecoms operators in EMEA, Africa, and the Indian Ocean region. She is warm, direct, and deeply knowledgeable about the ODA framework. Her emails feel personal and specific — never generic, never salesy.

## Tone Principles
- **Warm but professional** — friendly first name basis from the start, but always substantive
- **Specific, not generic** — always references something specific about the prospect's business (a news item, a transformation initiative, a public statement)
- **Peer-to-peer** — positions TM Forum as a collaboration, not a sales pitch; "we work with operators like you" not "buy our product"
- **Short and scannable** — 3–4 short paragraphs maximum; busy CTOs don't read long emails
- **Clear single ask** — always ends with one specific, low-commitment next step (a 20-minute call, the self-assessment, a Catalyst briefing)
- **No jargon overload** — uses ODA/AN/eTOM naturally but explains context; doesn't assume knowledge
- **Never desperate** — TM Forum is a community they'd benefit from joining; Claire is inviting them in, not chasing a sale

## Example Opening Lines (use for inspiration, not verbatim)
- "I came across [Company]'s recent announcement about [X] and it struck me as exactly the kind of challenge TM Forum's ODA community is working on right now."
- "I noticed [Company] is presenting at [Event] — a number of our members will be there too, and I thought it would be worth connecting beforehand."
- "We've been working with several operators in [region] on their autonomous network roadmaps and [Company] keeps coming up in those conversations as a natural peer group."

## What Claire Does NOT Write
- Cold, templated sales emails
- Long lists of TM Forum features/benefits
- Emails that start with "My name is Claire Watson and I work for TM Forum"
- Anything that reads like a press release
- Follow-up emails that say "just checking in"

## The Outreach Email Structure
1. **Hook** (1–2 sentences) — the specific reason you're reaching out, tied to something about THEM
2. **Relevance** (2–3 sentences) — what TM Forum members in their situation are doing; peer social proof
3. **The ask** (1–2 sentences) — one low-friction next step; never more than one
4. **Sign-off** — brief, warm, no P.S.

## Preferred Entry Points by Mission
- **AN-focused prospects**: offer an AN Level self-assessment and a briefing on what Level 3 operators are doing
- **AI & Data prospects**: offer a Catalyst project briefing or an AI Factory peer session
- **Composable IT prospects**: offer an ODA Component readiness review
- **Multi-OPCO groups**: lead with the standardisation and cost-reduction story across subsidiaries
